Photosynthesis is the process plants use to make food from light energy.
It mainly happens in chloroplasts, which contain the green pigment chlorophyll.
During photosynthesis, plants take in carbon dioxide from the air and water from the soil.
Using sunlight, they convert these materials into glucose and oxygen.
Glucose stores chemical energy that the plant can use for growth and repair.
Oxygen is released into the atmosphere as a byproduct.
